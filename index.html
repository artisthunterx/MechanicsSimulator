Initial speed Sometimes known as u and for this application you are too assume that u is always 0.
Final speed The formula for this is: v = u + at.
u is initial speed (= 0).
v is final speed.
t is time since being at the top of the slope.
a is acceleration (requires a different method of calculation, see below).
Force going down the hill The formula is: mg sin θ
m is the mass of the vehicle.
g is the acceleration due to gravity.
θ is the angle of the incline.
Frictional force going up the hill Frictional force is calculated by taking the chosen coefficient of friction and multiplying it by the reaction force to the surface of the incline.
Reaction force on the incline Reaction force on the surface is equivalent to: mg cos θ
m is mass
g is acceleration due to gravity
θ is the incline angle
θ This is generated when the game begins and is displayed below the animation window as θ = ...
Speed going down the hill This is a derived formula for speed: at
a is acceleration
t is time that has passed when the vehicle is at the point on the slope.
Distance down the hill For this we use one the equations of constant acceleration
s = ut + 0.5at2
...and since u = 0, s = 0.5at2.
